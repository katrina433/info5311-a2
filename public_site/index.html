Introduction
Our visualization and dataset is about capital punishment, also known as the death penalty. This is a method by which a person is deliberately killed as a punishment for a crime, generally following a state-authorized, rule-governed process to establish that the person has violated a norm-warranting punishment. The dataset includes variables related to executions in the US, including the number of executions, name of the perpetrator, perpetrator race, perpetrator sex, region, state, country, whether or not they are a juvenile, victim details, execution date, etc. We chose this dataset because it not only included details about the perpetrator and execution itself, but also adds some additional factors that might have contributed to their conviction and the courtâ€™s decision to punish the perpetrator with the death penalty.
